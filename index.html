HACKATHON #2

Austin, TX - May 9, 2014

We just finished our second hackathon this time it was a bit different there wasn't enough pressure like the first one, since whatever we start developing now will be the same as our final project. So we have about 3 weeks to work on implementing all features and come out with a complete functional app.

Our project is called FanClash and it is a fantasy sports like app, but instead of using the traditional daily fantasy format, it uses the pick-5 format. It consist on 10 selected players from a round of games for example the NBA playoffs have two games tonight the "Heat vs. Nets" and "Trail Blazers vs. Spurs", ten players will be selected from these two games and will be divided into 5 matchups of 2 players each for example "Lebron James vs. Tim Duncan" and 4 more matchups. The user will have to pick whoever they think will do better on each matchup for a total of 5 players selected. The players will receive a score based on their performance and stats on the game. The user who picks all correct will win, and in case of a tie there will be tiebreaker questions and whoever gets a the most accurate answer will be the winner.

The app is still in development and it won't have any real $ betting functionality until is fully tested and secured. It will also have a functionality to play with friends. The app is progressing fast and we are working hard in getting it to state that is fully functional to release the first version of it, but on the meantime if you want to see how it works and want to test it you can visit http://fan-clash.herokuapp.com/

HACKATHON #1

Austin, TX - Apr 12, 2014

So my first hackathon just finished a few days ago, and my team did really good. To be honest at the beginning I thought we were going to fail really bad, I thought our project was way out of our league. In 2 days we had to build an app called "Moodeo", with this app you are able to have a video call with a friend and both are able to watch a video at the same time, this will be a random video that will be selected from our database based on the user's mood (ex: If user selects the mood "funny", a funny video show for both users).

The hackathon started Monday at 9am and ended with presentations on Tuesday 5pm. We met on that Saturday before the hackathon trying to get everything in order, taking care of the planning, and the approaches that we were going to take. I have to admit even though we spend quite some time planning we would take long breaks sometimes to play "League of Legends" and clear our minds.

Monday arrived and the hackathon started. I must say that we did not have it easy, we were not able to use Rails for this app, we had to write all our backend code in Ruby using of course TDD (Test driven development), we had to have persistence in our data, and use HTML and CSS along with Sinatra to build the client interface. We had very little knowledge of how to add a persistent database, and also had no experience with APIs yet. For this project we also had to use an API called "Opentok" to help us with the video call functionality. We implemented this with Javascript to make the conections and Ruby to generated the session keys necessary to establish a connection between the users.

We did not get much rest before the start. The day the hackathon started I was fine, but after a full day of coding and writing over 1k lines of code between tests, use cases and methods for our app by 3am I was dead we finally ended up going to bed at 4am. Unfortunately even though I was extremely tired and running in autopilot, when I laid down in my bed, my brain was rolling at 10k RPM. I had at mix of random thoughts about 80% were code related but everything was unrelated and unorganized and I couldn't sleep.

I woke up at 7am got ready and went back to work on the app, I was very tired but at least regained some energy that helped me through the rest of the day, by around 12-1pm we had our back-end code about 90% finished, and probably about 75% for the front-end part. We still had to implement the main part of our app the video call between users and since we had never worked with APIs we had no idea on how to proceed. Short story we ended up reading a lot of documentation and experimenting with the API and finally about 30 mins before presentation we got it working. In the last 30 mins we had, we implemented it in our client interface and it was a success for the presentation. Even though we had a pretty good product and our presentation was pretty cool we did not have enough time to implement the part with the video playback based on users mood, but I must say what we delivered was pretty fuckin awesome, just by hearing the audience reaction when we tested the video call was good enough (literally I heard a few "Wow!!... What the fuck?!" from the people on the class before us).

Post hackathon mistakes...

So when the hackathon was over, we still had a beer keg for us to celebrate. I should have gone to sleep and recover, but I decided to stay and play many beer pong games. Up to this point I guess we had finished the keg and again I should have gone to get some rest, but no I went to play some League of Legends, stayed up to who knows what time playing and after that I was a zombie for the rest of the week, and some days of the week after, definitely going to limit if not stop playing LoL for good.

SXSW & AUSTIN ON RAILS

Austin, TX - Mar 12, 2014

So I was lucky enough to come to Austin by this time of the year I got to experience SXSW. SXSW, short for "South by Southwest", is an annual national music and film festival held in Austin, Texas. They feature major musical artists in a line-up along with film, "interactive" and comedy subdivisions. It is the largest music festival in the world.

Lucky me that the lady that I was staying with was a promoter for many bands coming so I got to go to many events for free. To put the cherry on top of the icing there was this event for Rails developers at Buffalo Billiards, hosted by an organization called Austin on Rails. I don't know how much this event costs but I got in for free courtesy of Makersquare.

This event was open bar and full of developers from everywhere, I met people from different states, and other countries like Argentina or the UK I got to play some ping pong with the developers at Lonely Planet. After a couple of beers I realized that the scotch was free as well FUCK YEAH!! (Black Label and above not that cheap Dewards crap). I forgot to mention that I also got free shirts from different tech companies. Free music events, free booze, free food, free developer events, and free shirts... can this get any better?

MAKERSQUARE

Austin, TX - Mar 8, 2014

What is MakerSquare? It's a school for full stack developers also referred to as a coding bootcamp. I think the term bootcamp is pretty accurate due to the intensity, discipline and dedication you have to put into it.

Before attending MakerSquare I had seen code before since I was an IT major I had been exposed to some code but nothing to deep and I tried to teach myself by reading books, following tutorials or online courses, and I did learn some stuff but there is a lot key things that I was missing. These key things include deep understanding of concepts, pair programming or working with a team, the importance of writing tests for our code, be a pro googler, and most important to think as a developer, among other things.

The decision was not easy as I had to quit my job and move from Florida to Texas, take out a loan, and rent a room in a house from a person I don't know. I guess later I will find out if all this was worth itor not, also due to the intensity of the program I wonder how many people will leave before the program is done?
